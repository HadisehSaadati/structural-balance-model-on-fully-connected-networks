# structural-balance-model-on-fully-connected-networks
Many studies of complex networks focus only on pairwise interactions. However, considering triadic relationships, where a third node influences the interaction between two others, allows us to study Structural Balance Theory. This project investigates how different initial network configurations affect the preservation of structural balance under perturbations.
Monte Carlo simulations based on the Metropolis algorithm were performed on three fully connected signed networks: all-positive (+1), all-negative (−1), and randomly signed networks. Structural balance was evaluated by monitoring the system's energy under different levels of thermal perturbation.
The results show that networks containing negative relationships lose their balanced structure at lower perturbation levels than all-positive networks. From a social-network perspective, this suggests that communities with initially cooperative relationships are more robust to disturbances, whereas communities dominated by hostility are significantly more fragile.
